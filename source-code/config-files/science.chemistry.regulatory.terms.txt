1. Spectral Analysis & TransformsFast Fourier Transform (FFT): Converts signals from the time domain to the frequency domain, allowing you to see which frequencies are most dominant.Discrete Cosine Transform (DCT): Similar to the FFT but uses only real numbers. Commonly used for lossy data compression.Hilbert Transform: Derives the analytical signal, useful for finding the instantaneous frequency and envelope of a signal.2. Digital FilteringFinite Impulse Response (FIR): Used for stable and precise linear-phase filtering.Infinite Impulse Response (IIR): Uses recursive feedback, requiring less computational power to achieve sharp frequency cutoffs.Matched Filtering: Used in radar, sonar, and communications to maximize the Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR) and detect known signals buried in noise.3. Modulation & DemodulationQuadrature Amplitude Modulation (QAM) & Phase Shift Keying (PSK): Manipulates signals at the mathematical level to encode and decode digital data into continuous waveforms (often used in software-defined radio).4. Mathematical & Statistical OperationsWindowing: Applies functions (like Hanning, Hamming, or Blackman) to signal blocks to minimize spectral leakage when performing Fourier transforms.Correlation & Convolution: Measures how two signals relate to one another, or how a system modifies a signal over time.Resampling & Decimation: Mathematically changes the sampling rate of a sequence to scale data appropriately without losing core information
